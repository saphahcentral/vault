TITLE: The Division of the Earth.

CODEX: OBSIDIAN CODEX.

EPOCH: AFTER THE FLOOD.

SECTION: THE DIVIDING OF THE NATIONS.

The generations after the flood divided the earth according to inheritance.

Yobelim 8 and 9 describe boundaries assigned to the sons of Noach.

The ancient divisions may not correspond perfectly to modern maps.

Directions preserved in ancient writings may have shifted through time.

Ancient west may correspond to parts of the modern southern hemisphere.

The Codex model proposes directional remapping after cataclysmic events.

The lands of Kanaan may once have extended into regions now forgotten.

Southern Africa may preserve fragments of ancient inheritance territories.

Mountains, rivers, and seas may have changed after global upheaval.

Human memory fragmented after the scattering of nations.

The knowledge of the original divisions became obscured.

The nations preserved distorted memories through myths and legends.

The division of the earth became both physical and spiritual.

Boundaries shaped the destinies of civilizations.

The scattering of languages accelerated the loss of ancient geography.

The Codex teaches that forgotten inheritances still influence mankind.
