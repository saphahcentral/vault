TITLE: The Fluid State of Meaning
CODEX: OC3-044
EPOCH: Epoch IV — The Pre-Form Layer
SECTION: Section E — Dissolution Fields

Meaning, once structured and defined, now becomes fluid.

It no longer holds a fixed position within a conceptual framework. Instead, it shifts continuously, adapting to the reader’s engagement and the surrounding field.

This fluidity is not instability.

It is adaptability.

The Codex is demonstrating that meaning is not inherent to any single form. It arises through interaction, shaped by context and perception.

The reader must abandon the expectation of permanence.

Interpretations that felt solid in earlier epochs now shift, expand, or dissolve entirely.

The Veil facilitates this fluidity by removing rigid filters.

Perception becomes more open, but also more demanding.

Clarity must be maintained without reliance on fixed structure.

This requires attention.

Not to what meaning is—

but to how it moves.
