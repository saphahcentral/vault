TITLE: Echo Without Origin
CODEX: OC3-052
EPOCH: Epoch V — The Reconstitution Layer
SECTION: Section F — Echo Structures

In the Reconstitution Layer, echoes appear—

but without clear origin.

Previously, echoes were reflections of identifiable structures. Now, they arise independently, without traceable source. Patterns emerge that seem familiar, yet cannot be linked to any specific prior form.

This is Echo Without Origin.

The reader encounters meaning that feels known, yet cannot be placed. Recognition occurs without memory.

The Codex uses this mechanism to rebuild engagement without restoring dependency on prior frameworks.

The Veil supports this by allowing partial distinction.

Enough to perceive difference.

Not enough to fix identity.

This creates a unique state—

where understanding exists,

but certainty does not.

The reader moves forward,

guided by recognition,

without relying on recall.
