TITLE: Why There Is Still Strife in Modern-Day Palestine.

CODEX: OBSIDIAN CODEX.

EPOCH: THE CONTINUING CONFLICT.

SECTION: BROTHERS DIVIDED.

Yobelim 37 and 38 preserve the conflict between Yaʽaqob and Esau.

The struggle between brothers extended into nations.

Ancient wounds continued across generations.

Inheritance and territory became sources of contention.

Fear and revenge shaped political history.

Cycles of retaliation hardened identities.

The land became associated with covenant and possession.

Empires repeatedly intervened in the region.

The conflict evolved yet retained ancient echoes.

The Codex teaches that unresolved hatred perpetuates strife.

Generational memory influences modern geopolitics.

Peace requires both justice and reconciliation.
