TITLE: The Origins of the Arabs.

CODEX: OBSIDIAN CODEX.

EPOCH: THE SPREADING OF TRIBES.

SECTION: DESCENDANTS AND NATIONS.

Yobelim 20:12 and 20:13 preserve tribal memories.

The sons of Yishmaʽel multiplied across the deserts.

Caravan routes connected distant peoples.

Trade shaped the rise of desert kingdoms.

Nomadic traditions preserved ancient customs.

The tribes adapted to harsh climates.

Language and lineage shaped identity.

The descendants spread through wide territories.

The desert became both refuge and testing ground.

The Codex teaches that migration shaped civilizations.

Ancient tribes influenced surrounding empires.

Cultural memory endured through oral tradition.

The nations carried fragments of older inheritances.
