TITLE: Names and Linguistic Distortion
CODEX: OC3-128
EPOCH: CURRENT AGE
SECTION: Section Q — Language Systems

Names change through transliteration across languages.

The original forms are often altered over time.

The letter J is a relatively recent addition to language systems.

Earlier names did not contain this letter.

This creates differences between original and modern pronunciations.

Translation and transliteration are frequently confused.

This leads to misidentification of names.

Understanding original forms requires linguistic awareness.
