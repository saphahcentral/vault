TITLE: The Eight-Day Week Misinterpretation
CODEX: OC3-124
EPOCH: CURRENT AGE
SECTION: Section M — Doctrinal Distortions

The concept of an eight-day week arises from a misinterpretation of symbolic language.

The established cycle of time consists of seven days forming a complete unit.

The eighth day represents transition or renewal rather than extension of the week.

Symbolic references are incorrectly treated as structural commands.

This leads to confusion in timekeeping and doctrinal understanding.

The original pattern of seven days remains consistent across ages.

The eighth day functions as a marker beyond completion.

This distinction preserves the integrity of the time cycle.
