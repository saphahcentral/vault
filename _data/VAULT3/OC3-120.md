TITLE: Directional Distortion Across Ages
CODEX: OC3-120
EPOCH: CROSS-AGE
SECTION: Section I — Spatial Interpretation

Directional references may not remain consistent across ages.

Geological shifts alter the orientation of land and features.

Catastrophic events can change spatial relationships.

Ancient descriptions must be interpreted with this in mind.

Modern mapping may not align directly with ancient accounts.

Relative positioning becomes more important than absolute direction.

This requires flexible interpretation of geographical data.

Such distortions explain apparent inconsistencies in descriptions.
