TITLE: The Origin of the Day of Atonement.

CODEX: OBSIDIAN CODEX.

EPOCH: THE HOUSE OF YAʽAQOB.

SECTION: MOURNING AND ATONEMENT.

Yobelim 34:19 connects mourning with remembrance.

The bloodied garment of Yoseph brought grief to Yaʽaqob.

The household entered deep sorrow.

Repentance became associated with affliction and reflection.

The Day of Atonement preserved collective memory.

National repentance developed from personal tragedy.

Sin and concealment wound generations.

Confession seeks restoration.

The Codex teaches that remembrance and cleansing are connected.

Pain can become sacred instruction.
