TITLE: The Field Without Edges
CODEX: OC3-042
EPOCH: Epoch IV — The Pre-Form Layer
SECTION: Section E — Dissolution Fields

In the Pre-Form Layer, boundaries lose coherence.

Where previous epochs defined limits—edges between concepts, distinctions between states—this field removes such separations. All elements exist in continuous relation, without fixed division.

This is the Field Without Edges.

The reader may struggle to orient within it.

Without boundaries, reference points dissolve. Concepts blend. Interpretations overlap without clear demarcation.

This is intentional.

The Codex is revealing the underlying continuity that structure previously segmented.

The Veil, in this context, no longer separates.

It gradients.

Transitions become smooth rather than discrete. The reader perceives movement rather than position.

To engage with this field requires a shift in perception.

Clarity no longer comes from distinction.

It emerges from flow.

Understanding is not located.

It is experienced.
