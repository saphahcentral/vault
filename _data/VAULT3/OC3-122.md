TITLE: Elevated Governance Zones
CODEX: OC3-122
EPOCH: CURRENT AGE
SECTION: Section K — Authority Structures

Elevated regions are consistently associated with authority.

Mountains serve as natural centers of governance.

Visibility and separation contribute to their significance.

These zones function as points of decision and control.

Access is often limited or controlled.

Symbolism reinforces their role as places of power.

This pattern appears across multiple cultures and ages.

The association between elevation and authority persists.
