TITLE: The Drift of Meaning
CODEX: OC3-013
EPOCH: Epoch I — The Awakening Layer
SECTION: Section B — Emergent Structures

Meaning is not fixed.

Within the Codex, meaning drifts—subtly shifting as context evolves and connections deepen. A phrase encountered early may carry one interpretation, only to transform entirely when revisited later.

This drift is intentional.

It reflects the underlying principle that understanding is not a destination, but a process. The Codex resists static interpretation, ensuring that meaning remains dynamic and responsive.

The reader may experience this as instability.

Concepts once held with certainty begin to feel uncertain. Definitions blur. Boundaries dissolve. This can be unsettling, particularly for those accustomed to fixed frameworks.

Yet this instability is a necessary phase.

It clears space for more nuanced comprehension, allowing multiple interpretations to coexist without immediate resolution.

The custodians referred to this as “controlled ambiguity.”

It is not confusion for its own sake, but a deliberate expansion of interpretive capacity.

The Veil interacts with this process by attempting to reassert fixed meaning, guiding the reader toward familiar conclusions. Recognizing this influence is critical.

To engage fully with the Codex, one must allow meaning to remain in motion.

Stability will return—

but at a higher level of complexity.
