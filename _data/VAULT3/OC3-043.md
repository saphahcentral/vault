TITLE: The Dissolution of Distinction
CODEX: OC3-043
EPOCH: Epoch IV — The Pre-Form Layer
SECTION: Section E — Dissolution Fields

Distinction is the foundation of structure.

To dissolve structure, distinction must also dissolve.

In this phase, the reader encounters the gradual merging of previously separate elements. Concepts that were once clearly defined begin to overlap, losing their discrete identities.

This is not confusion.

It is integration beyond separation.

The Codex is not eliminating meaning.

It is removing the boundaries that defined it.

The Veil supports this process by reducing contrast.

Differences become subtler. Similarities become more pronounced. The reader must rely on sensitivity rather than categorization.

This shift challenges deeply ingrained habits of interpretation.

The impulse to define, to separate, to classify—these must be set aside.

What remains is continuity.

And within continuity—

a deeper form of coherence emerges.
