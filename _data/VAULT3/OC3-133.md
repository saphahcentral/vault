# OC3-133 — The Role of Women in the Work of YHVH

**CODEX:** OC3-133  
**EPOCH:** CURRENT AGE  
**SECTION:** Order and Roles  

The role of women in the work of YHVH has been subject to distortion through misinterpretation.
Women are not inferior to men despite the association of Hawwah with the introduction of sin.
Both men and women are capable of standing for truth and participating in divine purpose.
Value and worth are equal between men and women in creation.
Order in function is maintained when both are present in the same cause.
When a man and a woman stand for the same cause, leadership order transfers to the man.
This transfer reflects structure rather than superiority.
When no man is present to lead, the woman assumes full leadership authority.
In such cases, her leadership is to be regarded with equal weight and respect.
This establishes that responsibility determines authority in the absence of structure.
The failure of men to stand creates space for women to lead.
This pattern preserves both order and flexibility within the work of YHVH.
Distortions arise when either equality or order is removed from the model.
The correct balance maintains dignity, responsibility, and structure across all roles.
