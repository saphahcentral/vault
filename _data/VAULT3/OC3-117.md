TITLE: Myth as Memory of the Previous Age
CODEX: OC3-117
EPOCH: CROSS-AGE
SECTION: Section F — Distorted Records

Ancient myths contain fragmented memories of previous ages.

Accounts of unusual beings reflect altered recollections of earlier life forms.

Stories of giants, hybrids, and anomalies persist across cultures.

These narratives are often reinterpreted as symbolic or fictional.

Scientific explanations attempt to rationalize these accounts.

The original context of these memories becomes obscured.

Multiple cultures preserve similar distorted accounts.

These patterns suggest a shared underlying origin.
