TITLE: The Seasons Beyond Earth.

CODEX: OBSIDIAN CODEX.

EPOCH: THE COSMIC EXPANSION.

SECTION: WORLDS AND CYCLES.

Earth shaped humanity through stable rhythms.

The seasons regulate agriculture and psychology.

Mars possesses longer seasonal cycles than Earth.

Martian winters endure for extended periods.

Dust storms can darken entire regions.

Lower gravity would alter human physiology.

Human circadian rhythms would slowly drift.

Jupiter's moons experience different environmental conditions.

Europa may conceal oceans beneath ice.

Ganymede possesses magnetic characteristics unusual among moons.

Artificial habitats may become necessary for survival.

Rogue planets and Planet X concepts involve extreme orbital cycles.

Some worlds may experience centuries of darkness.

Civilizations on such worlds would adapt differently.

Underground habitats may preserve life during cosmic winters.

Planet hopping through objects such as 3I/ATLAS may preserve civilization.

Humanity may one day migrate between worlds.

The Codex teaches that adaptation changes both body and mind.

Different skies produce different civilizations.
