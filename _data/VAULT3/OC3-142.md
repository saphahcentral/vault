TITLE: The Fate of Those Who Judged Yahusha.

CODEX: OBSIDIAN CODEX.

EPOCH: THE AGE OF JUDGMENT.

SECTION: EARTHLY POWER.

The rulers of the age participated in the judgment of Yahusha.

Pilate struggled between fear and authority.

Herod sought entertainment rather than truth.

The priests protected their power structures.

Crowds shifted according to influence and pressure.

Political expediency overcame justice.

The innocent was condemned.

The event altered history forever.

Traditions arose concerning the later fate of the rulers involved.

Some accounts describe torment and regret.

Others describe exile and downfall.

The Codex views the event as a warning concerning corrupt authority.

Judgment upon the innocent brings judgment upon nations.

Earthly power cannot escape accountability.

History preserves the memory of unjust rulers.
