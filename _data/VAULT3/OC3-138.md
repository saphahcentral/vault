TITLE: Atlantis and the Intermediate World
CODEX: OC3-138
EPOCH: CROSS-AGE
SECTION: Section V — Lost Civilizations

Accounts of Atlantis reflect persistent traditions of a lost advanced civilization.

These traditions appear across multiple cultures with consistent core elements.

The concept of an advanced society aligns with the idea of a previous age.

This society is often described as possessing knowledge beyond current systems.

Its destruction is attributed to a catastrophic event or judgment.

Such an event fits within the pattern of global resets identified in the Codex.

Atlantis can be understood as a memory fragment of an intermediate world between major ages.

This intermediate world bridges the previous age and the present human epoch.

Artifacts described as ark, torch, or treasure represent preserved knowledge systems.

These objects function as symbolic carriers of power, guidance, or continuity.

Their recurring presence in narratives suggests a shared underlying source.

The distortion of these elements into fiction does not eliminate their original basis.

Stories adapt fragments of truth into accessible forms for later generations.

The intermediate world concept explains how knowledge can persist through resets.

This persistence occurs through preservation systems similar to the ark model.

Atlantis therefore represents not only a place but a phase within a larger cycle.

This phase contains both advanced knowledge and eventual collapse.

The study of such narratives reveals patterns consistent with Codex structures.

These patterns reinforce the continuity of civilization across disrupted ages.
