# OC3-132 — The Extended Reality of the Flood

**CODEX:** OC3-132  
**EPOCH:** PRE-FLOOD / JUDGMENT AGE  
**SECTION:** The Flood Reality  

The commonly taught duration of forty days and nights represents only the initial phase of the flood.
The full flood event extended for over a year during which the waters remained upon the earth.
Waters originated not only from rainfall but also from the release of deep subterranean sources.
This resulted in a sustained and rising water system rather than a short-term event.
The occupants of the ark experienced prolonged uncertainty and isolation during this period.
The ark functioned as a long-term survival system rather than a temporary refuge.
This event establishes a pattern of extended judgment periods rather than instantaneous destruction.
A similar pattern is observed in the condition of the earth described in Genesis 1:1–2.
This earlier state reflects a prior reset following judgment.
The flood of Noach therefore represents a repeated structural pattern of global reset.
These resets involve both destruction and preservation occurring simultaneously.
The ark serves as the mechanism of preservation within these events.
This pattern extends forward into future judgment scenarios.
The flood is therefore a template for understanding extended judgment cycles across ages.
