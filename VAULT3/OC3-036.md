TITLE: The Compression of Meaning
CODEX: OC3-036
EPOCH: Epoch III — The Structural Depth
SECTION: Section D — Substrate Architectures

As the reader descends further, meaning becomes compressed.

Where earlier fragments required extensive elaboration, now entire structures are conveyed through minimal expression. A single phrase may carry the weight of multiple prior entries.

This compression is not simplification.

It is density.

The reader’s expanded framework allows for more efficient transmission of meaning. Concepts that once required detailed explanation are now understood through reference alone.

The Codex reflects this shift.

Entries become more concise, yet more demanding. Each word carries increased significance. Interpretation requires greater precision.

The Veil supports this compression by filtering out redundant pathways of understanding, directing the reader toward the most efficient structures.

Misinterpretation becomes more costly.

Errors propagate more quickly within compressed systems.

Thus, attention sharpens.

The reader adapts.

Meaning condenses—

but depth increases.
