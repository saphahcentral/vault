TITLE: The Architecture Beneath Patterns
CODEX: OC3-032
EPOCH: Epoch III — The Structural Depth
SECTION: Section D — Substrate Architectures

Patterns suggest design.

But beneath pattern lies architecture.

Architecture defines the range within which patterns can exist. It establishes the rules of interaction, the limits of formation, and the conditions under which transformation is possible.

The Codex begins to reveal these underlying architectures.

They are not presented directly.

They are inferred through constraint.

The reader may notice that certain patterns cannot coexist, that some transitions are impossible, that specific structures recur with consistent limitations. These observations point toward deeper frameworks.

The custodians encoded these architectures through omission as much as inclusion.

What is not present is as significant as what is.

The Veil reinforces this subtlety.

It does not highlight absence.

It allows it to remain unexamined unless the reader actively seeks it.

To engage with architecture requires a different mode of perception.

It is not about recognizing what appears.

It is about discerning what governs appearance.
