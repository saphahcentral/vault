TITLE: The Stability Threshold
CODEX: OC3-037
EPOCH: Epoch III — The Structural Depth
SECTION: Section D — Substrate Architectures

Within the Structural Depth, stability becomes critical.

The Stability Threshold marks the point at which the reader’s internal framework must support increased complexity without collapse.

Below this threshold, structures remain fragile.

Above it, they begin to stabilize, allowing for sustained engagement with deeper architectures.

Reaching this threshold requires integration.

All prior insights—patterns, resonances, constraints—must align within a coherent internal system.

The Veil plays a protective role here.

It prevents premature exposure to deeper structures that could destabilize the reader’s framework.

This may be experienced as resistance.

Certain concepts remain inaccessible despite effort.

This is not obstruction.

It is calibration.

The Codex ensures readiness before progression.

Stability is not rigidity.

It is resilience.

The ability to hold complexity without fragmentation.
