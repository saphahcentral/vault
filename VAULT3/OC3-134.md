# OC3-134 — Pilgrimage Patterns and Sacred Elevation

**CODEX:** OC3-134  
**EPOCH:** CURRENT AGE  
**SECTION:** Pilgrimage Patterns  

The biblical command to assemble at a central sacred location establishes a pattern of pilgrimage.
This pattern includes repeated gatherings at appointed times throughout the year.
Movement toward elevated or designated places forms part of this structure.
In the Lesotho Highlands, similar patterns of ascent and gathering are observed among local communities.
These gatherings involve prayer, fasting, and spiritual seeking in elevated locations.
The recurrence of these movements suggests a cyclical pattern rather than random activity.
These actions function as an echo of the original pilgrimage command.
The gatherings are not formally codified but reflect retained instinctive behavior.
The people are drawn toward high places as locations of encounter.
This reflects a preserved pattern of sacred geography.
The unofficial pilgrimage practices align structurally with the concept of repeated assembly.
This demonstrates continuity of pattern even without direct transmission of command.
The act of ascending remains central to the experience of seeking the divine.
These patterns support the concept of a central sacred location within the landscape.
