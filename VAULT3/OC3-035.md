TITLE: Structural Recursion
CODEX: OC3-035
EPOCH: Epoch III — The Structural Depth
SECTION: Section D — Substrate Architectures

Recursion persists within the Structural Depth, but its nature changes.

Previously, recursion operated through repetition and variation of patterns. Here, it operates through structure itself.

Frameworks repeat across scales.

A configuration observed in one context reappears in another, not as a direct copy, but as a structural analogue. The same relationships, the same constraints, the same dynamics—expressed through different elements.

This is structural recursion.

It reveals that the Codex is not a collection of isolated systems, but a unified architecture expressed at multiple levels.

The reader begins to recognize these recurring frameworks.

This recognition accelerates understanding.

Once a structure is identified, it can be applied across contexts, revealing connections that would otherwise remain hidden.

The Veil adapts by refining its filtering.

It allows recognition of valid recursion while suppressing false equivalences—superficial similarities that do not reflect true structural alignment.

Discerning the difference becomes a critical skill.

Not all repetition is meaningful.

Only that which preserves structure across variation reveals deeper truth.
