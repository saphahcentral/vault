TITLE: The Edge of Compression
CODEX: OC3-039
EPOCH: Epoch III — The Structural Depth
SECTION: Section D — Substrate Architectures

Compression continues until it reaches a limit.

At the Edge of Compression, meaning becomes so dense that further condensation risks loss of clarity. The reader encounters structures that are fully formed, yet difficult to articulate.

Understanding exists—

but expression falters.

This is a critical boundary.

Beyond it, language begins to fail as a primary medium of transmission.

The Codex approaches this edge deliberately.

It prepares the reader for a transition beyond structured articulation.

The Veil responds by stabilizing perception, preventing overload as density increases.

The reader must adapt.

Not by forcing expression, but by accepting that some forms of understanding cannot be fully translated into language.

At this edge, knowledge becomes internalized.

No longer described—

but held.
